üìñ Case Study: Safety Incident at TechNow Solutions

The Scenario

TechNow Solutions is a small IT company with 35 employees located in a two-story office building. On Monday morning at 10:30 AM, a significant safety incident occurred that affected multiple employees.

Background Information

TechNow Solutions has recently moved to a new office space. The company has a health and safety coordinator, Maria, who was hired three months ago. The office layout is as follows:

  • Ground Floor: Reception, conference rooms, kitchen area
  • First Floor: Open office space, server room, IT support area
  • Emergency exits: Main entrance and a side fire exit (occasionally blocked by storage boxes)
  • Fire extinguishers: Located near the kitchen and server room only

The Incident

On Monday morning, an electrical fire started in the server room on the first floor. Here's what happened:

  • Employee James, working nearby, noticed smoke and immediately alerted his colleagues.
  • The fire alarm was activated, but several employees were confused about evacuation procedures.
  • One employee, Sarah, attempted to use the fire extinguisher but had never been trained on its proper use.
  • The side emergency exit was blocked by storage boxes, forcing people to use only the main entrance.
  • An elderly employee, Robert, had difficulty evacuating due to mobility issues and received assistance from a colleague.
  • The fire brigade arrived 8 minutes after the 911 call and quickly extinguished the fire.
  • Two employees suffered minor smoke inhalation, but no serious injuries occurred.

Aftermath

After the incident, an investigation was conducted. Maria realized that several health and safety measures were not properly implemented despite being company policy.

üîë Key Terms to Consider

  • Hazard: A source of potential harm or adverse health effect
  • Risk: The likelihood that a hazard will cause harm
  • Control Measure: Actions taken to reduce or eliminate a risk
  • Evacuation Procedure: The planned method for safely leaving a building during an emergency
  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Equipment worn for protection against health and safety risks
